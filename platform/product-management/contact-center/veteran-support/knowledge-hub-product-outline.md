# Product Outline: Veteran Support Knowledge Hub & Documentation Governance

## Overview
The Veteran Support Knowledge Hub is a centralized documentation ecosystem designed to improve access, consistency, and governance of operational knowledge across Veteran Support.

This initiative consolidates documentation currently distributed across GitHub, SharePoint, Platform Pages, Confluence, and Teams into a structured system with defined source-of-truth standards and ownership.

---

## Problem Statement
Veteran Support documentation is fragmented across multiple platforms with no standardized structure or governance model.

This results in:
- Difficulty locating accurate and up-to-date information
- Duplicate and outdated documentation across systems
- Increased dependency on Veteran Support for guidance
- Inconsistent onboarding and knowledge transfer
- Inefficiencies in cross-team collaboration

---

## Objectives
- Define clear source-of-truth locations for documentation
- Create a centralized Knowledge Hub entry point
- Standardize documentation structure and taxonomy
- Establish governance for documentation ownership and lifecycle
- Improve discoverability and usability of Veteran Support resources

---

## Users / Stakeholders

### Primary Users
- VA.gov Product Teams
- Engineering Teams
- Veteran Support (Tier II / Tier III)

### Secondary Stakeholders
- Platform Team
- OCTO Leadership
- Data / Insights Teams (Medallia, DOMO, etc.)

---

## Key Capabilities
- Centralized Knowledge Hub (single entry point)
- Source-of-truth mapping (GitHub vs SharePoint vs Platform Pages)
- Standardized documentation structure and taxonomy
- Governance model (ownership, updates, lifecycle)
- Improved discoverability and navigation

---

## Scope

### In Scope
- Documentation inventory and audit across platforms
- Source-of-truth framework definition
- Knowledge Hub structure and taxonomy design
- Migration of high-priority documentation
- Governance standards and documentation lifecycle definition

### Out of Scope
- Full rewrite of all documentation content
- Platform/tool replacement
- Automation and advanced search (future phase)

---

## Success Metrics (KPIs)

### Quantitative (where possible)
- Reduction in repeat inquiries to Veteran Support
- Decrease in time-to-resolution for support requests
- Increased usage of self-service documentation

### Qualitative
- Improved onboarding experience for new team members
- Positive feedback on ease of finding information
- Reduced confusion around escalation and engagement processes

---

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Lack of alignment on source-of-truth decisions | Engage stakeholders early and validate framework before migration |
| Resistance to changing documentation habits | Provide clear guidance and intuitive structure |
| Documentation becomes outdated over time | Establish governance and ownership model |
| Access limitations across platforms | Validate permissions during implementation |

---

## Dependencies
- Stakeholder alignment (Platform, Product, Support teams)
- Access to all documentation repositories
- Collaboration with document owners (where applicable)

---

## Phased Approach

### Phase 1 — Discovery & Inventory
- Audit existing documentation across platforms
- Identify duplication, gaps, and ownership

### Phase 2 — Definition & Design
- Define source-of-truth framework
- Design Knowledge Hub structure and taxonomy

### Phase 3 — Implementation
- Create Knowledge Hub entry point
- Migrate high-priority documentation
- Organize content into defined structure

### Phase 4 — Governance & Sustainment
- Define ownership model
- Establish update cadence and standards
- Document ongoing maintenance process
