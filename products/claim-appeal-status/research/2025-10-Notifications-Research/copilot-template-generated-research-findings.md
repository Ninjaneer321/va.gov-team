# CoPilot Templated: Benefit Notifications Research Findings

**Office of the CTO - Digital Experience (OCTO-DE), VA Claim and Appeal Status Tool - Notifications, Benefits Management Tools (BMT 3)**
**Date:** 01/09/2026
**Contacts:** Naomi Howe, Melissa Lefevre

---

## Research Goals Section

1. **Journey context:** Supports Veterans during "Taking Care of Myself" (managing ongoing disability compensation and appeals) and "Putting Down Roots" (maintaining access to benefits and records for financial stability), per the VA Veteran Journey Map.
2. **Key moments:** 
   - Receiving decision letters about claims
   - Being asked to submit additional evidence for claims
   - Monitoring ongoing claim status
3. **Intended outcomes:** 
   - Reduce time to act on evidence requests
   - Increase user understanding of notification purpose and urgency
   - Improve trust and engagement with VA digital communications
4. **Guiding questions:** 
   - "How do Veterans interpret and respond to decision letter and evidence request notifications across digital channels?"
   - "What information do Veterans expect and need in each channel?"
   - "What factors influence Veterans’ trust in official VA notifications?"

---

## Research Questions Section

- How do Veterans interpret and respond to decision letter and evidence request notifications across email, text, and in-app?
- How much information feels appropriate in each channel for both message types?
- How do tone and message structure affect clarity, trust, and urgency?
- How do Veterans decide which channel to engage with first?
- How can VA ensure decision letters and evidence requests are clear, actionable, and trustworthy across digital channels?

---

## Methodology Section

- **Moderated Interviews (N=12):** Each 60-min remote session evaluated sample notifications from email, text, and VA app push, discussing trust, clarity, urgency, and next steps. Scenario-based: reviewed both decision letter and evidence request notifications in each channel.
- **Device Usage:** Mobile for initial review; desktop/laptop for more complex actions (e.g., uploading evidence).
- **Recruitment:** Veterans actively using VA.gov or VA Health and Benefits app, with recent experience receiving notifications.

---

## Hypotheses and Conclusions Section

- **Hypothesis:** Veterans prefer email for formal notifications and text or in-app for urgent items.
  - **Conclusion:** Likely True.
  - Evidence: Email rated highest for trust; action-required items liked in all channels but urgency/clarity cues needed.
- **Hypothesis:** Clear branding and .gov URLs drive trust.
  - **Conclusion:** Definitely True.
  - Evidence: All participants cited logos, familiar sender info, and .gov domains as trust cues.
- **Hypothesis:** Veterans are unclear about opt-in/opt-out controls for notifications.
  - **Conclusion:** Definitely True.
  - Evidence: 8/12 expressed confusion about STOP/START for SMS or how to restart notifications.
  - Quote: **P16:** "How would you expect to be able to manage your VA notification preferences? I don't know..."
- **Hypothesis:** Staggered, multi-channel delivery increases uptake.
  - **Conclusion:** Likely True.
  - Evidence: Majority liked receiving multiple notifications (email+text) for important tasks.

---

## Key Findings Section

1. **VA Messages Often Feel Urgent—Clear Deadlines Make Them Actionable.** Veterans treat all VA messages as important, but specific timeframes and instructions for evidence requests ensure quick responses.
2. **Trust in VA Notifications varies by Channel and Familiarity.** Email is most trusted; push notifications gain trust with app familiarity; texts viewed with skepticism/scam concern.
3. **Consistent VA Branding and Digital Identifiers Are Essential for Trust.** Visual branding (.gov, logos, familiar numbers) is critical for confidence, reducing scam fears.
4. **Preference for Navigating Directly to VA.gov or App for Security.** Users often avoid clicking links, going directly to VA.gov/app for authentication and safety.
5. **Multi-channel delivery is welcomed, especially for action-required items.** Veterans like receiving notifications through multiple channels, especially staggered for urgent tasks.
6. **Veterans Switch Devices for Task Complexity.** Simple actions on mobile; evidence upload or complex steps on desktop/laptop.
7. **Confusion Persists Around Notification Preferences, SMS Opt-Out.** Uncertainty on stopping/restarting SMS and notification settings causes missed or unwanted messages.
8. **Communication clarity is strong except evidence request texts.** 100% clarity for decision letter email/text, but only 64% for evidence request texts due to missing deadlines.
9. **Preferences Split on Claim Status Frequency.** Some want periodic updates; others prefer only when claims progress. All agree updates are beneficial if customization is possible.
10. **Appropriate Level of Detail Reassures Privacy Concerns.** Veterans approve general language in notifications, prefer privacy-preserving content and customizable channel choices.

---

## Details of Findings Section

### Finding 1: VA Messages Often Feel Urgent—Clear Deadlines Make Them Actionable

Veterans interpret any contact from VA as high priority. However, actionable requests (especially for evidence) require clear, visible deadlines to prompt fast responses.

- _Supporting data: **P6:** "I wish it would say, please respond to the request by such-and-such date." (interview, 2025-12-12)_
- _Supporting data: 9 of 12 participants said deadlines made a difference in how quickly they responded._
- _Supporting data: Decision letter notifications were perceived as urgent, but evidence requests without deadlines were often ignored or delayed._

### Finding 2: Trust in VA Notifications varies by Channel and Familiarity

Email feels safest due to established filtering; push notifications are trusted if the user is familiar with the VA app. Texts raise scam skepticism, especially with unfamiliar short codes.

- _Supporting data: **P16:** "Because I've never really received a push notification from the VA, I'm leery."_
- _Supporting data: 12/12 said email is their most trusted channel for benefits notifications._

### Finding 3: Consistent VA Branding and Digital Identifiers Are Essential

Visual cues like the VA logo, .gov URLs, and familiar short codes drive trust.

- _Supporting data: **P5:** "It's got the VA logo. That makes it legitimate."_
- _Supporting data: All participants checked .gov URLs and sender numbers before acting._

### Finding 4: Direct Navigation Preferred for Security

Veterans often bypass embedded links, choosing to go straight to va.gov or the app for peace of mind.

- _Supporting data: **P15:** "I'm not going to click that link. I would go to VA.gov and log in."_
- _Supporting data: Majority cited phishing risk as reason for this behavior._

### Finding 5: Multi-channel Delivery Welcomed

Receiving notifications in multiple channels is seen as helpful; staggered timing for evidence requests preferred as extra reminders.

- _Supporting data: 10/12 preferred notifications via more than one channel for urgent matters._
- _Supporting data: **P1:** "They are all telling me the same thing to do... I'd go look at them and see what they was about."_

### Finding 6: Device Switching for Complex Tasks

Veterans move from phone to desktop/laptop for submitting evidence or completing lengthy forms.

- _Supporting data: **P5:** "Uploading a document on the phone might be more of a challenge... as we're getting older is easier on a bigger screen."_

### Finding 7: Confusion About Notification Preferences and SMS Opt-Out

Many are unclear how to stop or restart notifications; “Text STOP” feels ambiguous.

- _Supporting data: **P16:** "How would you expect to manage your VA notification preferences? I don't know..."_
- _Supporting data: Only 2 out of 12 understood how to resume texts after sending STOP._

### Finding 8: Clarity High Except Evidence Request Texts

Clarity for text/email decision letter notifications was rated near 100%; only 7/11 found evidence request texts sufficiently detailed.

- _Supporting data: 91% clarity for decision letter email/push; 64% for evidence request text._
- _Supporting data: Lack of specific deadlines decreased perceived urgency and completion rates._

### Finding 9: Split Preferences on Status Update Frequency

Some desire regular claim status notifications, others prefer only updates on change.

- _Supporting data: **P17:** "Would like claim status notifications periodically even if status hasn't changed."_
- _Supporting data: **P4:** "Notify only when there is an update, not every week."_

### Finding 10: Detail Level Protects Privacy

Veterans value succinct language and lack of sensitive information in notifications, especially when using shared devices.

- _Supporting data: **P8:** "I don't want to see more information on there because of device sharing. I like that it's general."_

---

## Additional Insights Section

- **Multi-channel helps reduce missed responses:** Staggered or repeated notifications increase completion.
- **Preference for opt-in/out guidance:** Participants want plain instructions for re-enabling notifications after opting out.
- **Trust signals vary across demographics:** Familiarity with VA app and mobile technology impacts notification trust.

---

## Recommendations Section

**High Priority:**
1. Always include explicit deadlines or “respond by” dates in evidence requests.
   - *Addresses Finding 1; 9/12 requested deadline cues for urgent action.*
2. Standardize urgent message formatting with clear language, bold headers, and icons for action-required messages.
   - *Addresses Finding 8; clarity and urgency drive faster task completion.*
3. Strengthen security cues for SMS: Use recognizable VA sender info, short codes, branding, and .gov URLs.
   - *Addresses Finding 2 & 3; reduces skepticism, boosts trust.*

**Medium Priority:**
4. Educate users on identifying authentic VA communications—provide trust cues (logos, domain, sender ID) in onboarding and reminders.
5. Improve notification management: Provide stepwise, channel-specific opt-out and re-subscription instructions (e.g., “Text STOP/START” in SMS, detailed help in profile).
6. Continue supporting all channels (email, SMS, push); let users customize per channel.

**Low Priority:**
7. Promote cross-device flexibility in notifications; encourage users to "continue actions on desktop" for complex tasks.
8. Monitor opt-in and open rates to personalize notification cadence and avoid fatigue.

## Product User and Business Outcomes Section

### Desired User Outcome
Enable Veterans to review actions and respond to claim or evidence requests promptly across preferred channels.

- Findings show deadlines and urgency cues improve prompt response.
- Recommendations for clear urgency and cross-channel delivery support user task completion.

### Desired Business Outcome
Increase claim evidence response rates and completion of action-required tasks.

- Clarity and urgency-driven communications, paired with multi-channel notifications, will increase timely evidence submission.
- Improved opt-in/out management and security cues will reduce missed actions and build trust.

---

## Key Performance Indicators Section

### KPI 1: Evidence Submission Completion Rate
Clear deadlines and urgent cues in evidence request messages; measure increased completion rates post-implementation.

### KPI 2: Notification Engagement Rate
Monitor open and engagement rates by channel (email vs. SMS vs. push); use split data to adjust templates for trust and clarity.

### KPI 3: Opt-in/Opt-out Handling
Track opt-out/re-subscription rates and profile management activity, aiming for reduced missed messages and improved preference control.

## Further Research Needed Section

### Research Gaps

- **Detailed impact of multi-channel overload:** Further study how notification frequency and timing impact response and fatigue.
- **Demographic differences in channel trust:** Explore how age or digital literacy modifies trust signals.
- **Accessibility and device sharing:** Address unique needs for Veterans using shared or assistive devices.

### Underserved Groups Not Yet Studied

- **Rural Veterans:** Only 2 of 12; more representation needed.
- **Veterans over 65:** Only 2 of 12.
- **Veterans with cognitive/hearing disabilities:** 1 cognitive, 0 hearing disability; targeted recruitment recommended.
- **Non-English speakers:** 0 recruited; translation and language accessibility not assessed.
