## Key Findings At a Glance

### 🔴 CRITICAL Issues (Must Fix)
1. **Terminology Confusion** (9/11 participants)
   - "Overpayment balance" is ambiguous and confusing
   - Should say "Amount Due" or "Outstanding Debt"

2. **No Visibility/Tracking** (8/11 participants)
   - Veterans can't see current balance or payment progress
   - Can't access debt tracking in VA.gov

3. **Multiple Confusing Letters** (7/11 participants)
   - First letter vague, second letter weeks later with details
   - Causes anxiety and requires phone calls to clarify

### 🟡 MAJOR Issues (Should Fix)
4. **Want Online Over Phone** (6/11 participants)
5. **Information Architecture** (5/11 participants) - Buried info, too many clicks
6. **Need Receipts/Confirmations** (4/11 participants)
7. **VA's Own Errors** (4/11 participants) - VA slowness created debts

### 🟢 What Works Well
- Automatic payments liked (6/11) - "set and forget" reduces stress
- Prototype was positively received (P13) - clear options, good visual design
- Mobile apps preferred over websites for simplicity

## Most Important Verbatim Quotes

### On Terminology
**P02**: "Overpayment means you made a mistake, you overpaid me. Why do I owe you money back? That's your mistake, not mine."

### On Tracking
**P09**: "I can't go into VA.gov and track it. I would have to go to Deers.com or another totally different email or different app. I can't go into VA.gov."

### On Multiple Letters
**P16**: "I got a letter saying I was overpaid and they'd have to get it back... what does that mean? Am I not gonna get paid one month? I was very stressed."

### On Online Preference
**P06**: "Maybe if there was a website I could go to instead of having to call? Definitely want to figure out how to do things online or maybe with an app."

### On Trust
**P02**: "I don't trust that they will take my payment, apply it to my debt and provide me a fresh balance every month."
