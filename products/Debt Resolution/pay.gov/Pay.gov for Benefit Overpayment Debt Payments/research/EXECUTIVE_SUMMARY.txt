VA.GOV PAY.GOV DEBT PAYMENT RESEARCH - EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
11 Participants | UX Research Study
================================================================================

PARTICIPANT OVERVIEW:
- 8 Desktop, 2 Mobile, 1 Smartphone users
- 9 on payment plans, 2 not yet
- 7 experiencing financial hardship
- Age range: 25-69
- Most common debts: Disability compensation/pension overpayments, GI Bill overpayments

================================================================================
CRITICAL FINDINGS (Must Fix)
================================================================================

1. TERMINOLOGY CONFUSION - Affects 9/11 participants
   - "Overpayment balance" is ambiguous and confusing
   - Veterans interpret "overpayment" as VA's mistake, not their debt
   - "Balance" implies credit, not debt owed
   - SOLUTION: Use "Amount Due" or "Outstanding Debt"

   KEY QUOTE (P02):
   "Overpayment means you made a mistake, you overpaid me. Why do I owe you 
   money back? That's your mistake, not mine."

2. NO VISIBILITY/TRACKING - Affects 8/11 participants  
   - Veterans pay automatically but can't see current balance
   - No way to track payment progress toward payoff
   - Can't see payment history or when debt will be cleared
   - Can't access this info in VA.gov (must go to separate systems like DEERS)
   - SOLUTION: Add debt tracking dashboard to VA.gov showing:
     * Original overpayment amount
     * Current balance
     * Payment history with dates
     * Projected payoff date
     * Monthly deduction amount

   KEY QUOTE (P09):
   "I can't go into VA.gov and track it. I would have to go to Deers.com or 
   another totally different email or different app. I can't go into VA.gov."

3. MULTIPLE CONFUSING LETTERS - Affects 7/11 participants
   - First letter: "You were overpaid, we'll get it back" (vague, scary)
   - Second letter (weeks later): "Here's the repayment plan"
   - Causes anxiety and requires phone call to clarify
   - SOLUTION: Send ONE consolidated letter with:
     * Clear explanation of what debt is and why
     * All repayment options clearly laid out
     * If automatic: deduction amount, start date, end date, frequency
     * Contact info for questions or financial hardship

   KEY QUOTE (P16):
   "I got a letter saying I was overpaid and they'd have to get it back... 
   what does that mean? Am I not gonna get paid one month? I was very stressed."

================================================================================
MAJOR ISSUES (Should Fix)
================================================================================

4. PREFER ONLINE OVER PHONE - 6/11 participants wanted self-service
   - Currently must call VA to set up payment plans
   - Veterans prefer apps and websites
   - Would like "financial assistant" tool online to set plan without phone call

5. INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE - 5/11 mention buried/hard-to-find info
   - Essential information requires multiple clicks
   - Key details not front-facing (file number, name, verification info)
   - Too many clicks to reach payment section

6. WANT RECEIPTS/CONFIRMATIONS - 4/11 participants
   - Want downloadable PDF receipt after payment
   - Want to confirm payment was applied correctly
   - Want email notification of payment received

7. VA'S OWN ERRORS CAUSED DEBTS - 4/11 participants 
   - P04: VA took 10 months to process dependent disenrollment paperwork
   - P06: VA was slow processing education overpayment
   - Veterans resent having to repay VA's administrative mistakes
   - PERCEPTION: "They're fast at collecting debt but slow at fixing mistakes"

================================================================================
POSITIVE FINDINGS (What Works)
================================================================================

AUTOMATIC PAYMENTS ARE LIKED (6/11)
- "Set and forget" reduces mental burden
- Veterans prefer not thinking about payments

PROTOTYPE SHOWED PROMISE (P13)
- Clear presentation of options
- Visual design (bold text, checkmarks) was easy to read
- Ability to see different payment methods helped confidence
- Participant P13 rated experience as "seamless" and "easy"

MOBILE APP USERS PREFER APPS (P06)
- Simple interface with 4 tabs
- Easy to read/visible text
- Direct to information
- Could model VA debt payment app after this

================================================================================
KEY RECOMMENDATIONS (IN PRIORITY ORDER)
================================================================================

IMMEDIATE (Sprint 1-2):
1. Change terminology from "overpayment balance" to "Amount Due" or "Outstanding Debt"
2. Add debt tracking to VA.gov showing balance, payment history, payoff date
3. Consolidate multiple letters into one with complete information

SHORT-TERM (Sprint 3-4):
4. Provide downloadable PDF receipt after payment
5. Send email confirmation of payment received and applied
6. Add ability to adjust payment amount online (not just on phone)

MEDIUM-TERM (Sprint 5+):
7. Create online payment plan setup (financial assessment tool)
8. Develop mobile app for debt management
9. Improve information architecture (front-load key info, reduce clicks)

================================================================================
CONFIDENCE & TRUST ISSUES
================================================================================

Some veterans expressed distrust:
- P02: "I don't trust they will apply my payment correctly to my debt"
- This stems directly from lack of visibility/tracking

Providing transparent tracking dashboard would directly address this concern.
