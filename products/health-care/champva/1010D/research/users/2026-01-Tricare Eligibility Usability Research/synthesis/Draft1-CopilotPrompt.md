## 4. THE 5 WHYS ANALYSIS

### **Pain Point 1: Participants Don't Know What CHAMPVA Is**

1. **Why are participants unfamiliar with CHAMPVA?**
   - They've never heard of the program or seen marketing for it

2. **Why haven't they encountered CHAMPVA before?**
   - Limited outreach/awareness campaigns; information only shared at specific touchpoints (e.g., caregiver program)

3. **Why is outreach limited to specific touchpoints?**
   - Program may be targeting based on assumed triggers (veteran disability, caregiving) rather than broad family member audience

4. **Why is targeting so narrow?**
   - Assumption that family members will seek out insurance when needed, rather than proactively educating potential beneficiaries

5. **ROOT CAUSE**: Reactive rather than proactive communication strategy; lack of broad public awareness campaigns for CHAMPVA

---

### **Pain Point 2: Cannot Differentiate CHAMPVA from TRICARE**

1. **Why can't users tell the difference?**
   - Programs have similar names and both relate to military/VA healthcare

2. **Why do similar names cause confusion?**
   - Both use military-adjacent naming (TRICARE, CHAMPVA); don't clearly indicate purpose or audience

3. **Why don't names indicate purpose?**
   - Acronyms and legacy naming conventions prioritized over user-centered language

4. **Why are legacy naming conventions still used?**
   - Organizational/legal requirements; historical program establishment

5. **ROOT CAUSE**: Government naming conventions and acronyms not designed with end-user comprehension as primary goal; lack of plain language branding

---

### **Pain Point 3: Incorrect Understanding of Eligibility**

1. **Why do users have incorrect eligibility understanding?**
   - Making assumptions based on partial information or other programs (TRICARE)

2. **Why are they relying on assumptions?**
   - No clear, accessible source of truth they've encountered before applying

3. **Why haven't they seen clear eligibility criteria?**
   - Information may exist but not surfaced at the right time/place in user journey

4. **Why isn't information surfaced effectively?**
   - Eligibility logic is complex (dependent on veteran status, service connection, disability rating, other insurance) and hard to communicate simply

5. **ROOT CAUSE**: Complex eligibility rules that are difficult to communicate in simple, actionable terms; information architecture doesn't surface key details early enough

---

### **Pain Point 4: Users Choose TRICARE by Default**

1. **Why do users default to TRICARE?**
   - They're familiar with it and know it works

2. **Why are they familiar with TRICARE but not CHAMPVA?**
   - TRICARE covers broader population (active duty, retirees) while CHAMPVA serves niche audience

3. **Why doesn't CHAMPVA reach its audience effectively?**
   - Eligibility is narrower (family members of 100% P&T veterans or survivors) and outreach is limited

4. **Why is outreach limited to this specific audience?**
   - Resource constraints and assumption this audience will seek out benefits

5. **ROOT CAUSE**: Underestimation of need for proactive education; assumption that motivated beneficiaries will navigate complex eligibility on their own

## OUTPUT SUMMARY

### **1. Executive Summary**

- **Critical Knowledge Gap**: 70% of participants had never heard of CHAMPVA despite all being familiar with TRICARE
- **Program Confusion**: 90% could not clearly differentiate CHAMPVA from TRICARE, with some holding completely incorrect assumptions
- **Eligibility Uncertainty**: Only 1 participant (P14) could accurately explain CHAMPVA eligibility
- **Systemic Communication Failure**: Users discover CHAMPVA accidentally through social media, friends, or unrelated VA programs rather than proactive outreach
- **Incomplete Data**: Full usability findings from prototype testing not visible in transcript excerpts; requires access to complete session data

---

### **2. Key Insights (Numbered with Evidence)**

1. **CHAMPVA has a severe awareness problem among its target audience**
   - Evidence: 7/10 participants had never heard of the program; P16 only learned of it the morning of the study via Facebook

2. **TRICARE serves as the dominant mental model for VA health insurance**
   - Evidence: 10/10 participants recognized TRICARE; participants consistently used TRICARE as reference point to understand other programs

3. **Program naming conventions create confusion rather than clarity**
   - Evidence: P16 thought "CHAMPVA" referred to AI/automation for claims; multiple participants couldn't distinguish programs by name alone

4. **Users make benefit decisions with incomplete information**
   - Evidence: P03 chose TRICARE for Life over CHAMPVA citing "better benefits" but couldn't explain CHAMPVA; suggests comparison without full understanding

5. **Eligibility criteria are too complex for self-service understanding**
   - Evidence: Even P14, the most knowledgeable participant, provided only partial eligibility description; others resorted to vague generalizations or admitted not knowing

6. **Current discovery methods are inadequate and inconsistent**
   - Evidence: P16 (Facebook), P03 (caregiver program), P15 (friend), P16 (social media) - all accidental, informal, or secondary channels

7. **Users need eligibility confirmation BEFORE starting the application**
   - Evidence: Multiple participants uncertain if they would qualify; moderator had to explain research wasn't testing their actual eligibility

8. **Plain language and visual comparisons are needed for program differentiation**
   - Evidence: Verbal explanations inadequate (P15: "No, I couldn't"); participants struggled with abstract descriptions

9. **Word-of-mouth information is incomplete and sometimes incorrect**
   - Evidence: P15 learned from friend but couldn't explain details; P06 had completely reversed understanding of TRICARE vs. CHAMPVA eligibility

10. **CHAMPVA's value proposition is not widely understood**
    - Evidence: Only P14 articulated the "gap-filling" role of CHAMPVA for families without TRICARE access

- **Impact**: Improved comprehension, fewer errors

## CONCLUSION

This research reveals a **critical systemic failure in CHAMPVA awareness and education**. Despite serving an important need for families of 100% permanently and totally disabled veterans, the program is virtually unknown to its target audience. Those who have heard of it cannot distinguish it from TRICARE or explain eligibility criteria accurately.

The root causes are:
1. **Reactive vs. proactive communication strategy**
2. **Complex eligibility criteria difficult to self-assess**
3. **Confusing program naming and positioning**
4. **Lack of integrated family member benefit information**

**The most impactful interventions will be:**
- **Pre-application eligibility tools** to guide users to the right program
- **Proactive notification** when families become eligible
- **Clear comparative content** positioning CHAMPVA relative to TRICARE
- **Plain language** throughout the application experience
