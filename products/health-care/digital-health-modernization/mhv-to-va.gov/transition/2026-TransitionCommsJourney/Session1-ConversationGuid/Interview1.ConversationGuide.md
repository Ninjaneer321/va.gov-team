# **Conversation Guide  (3/16 - 19 after Appointments warning, T-60 to T- 30 changes)**


## **Check-in 1: Baseline + Early Exposure (30 minutes)**


## **Moderator logistics**


### **Logistics to confirm periodically**

Check Settings -> Meeting -> In Meeting (Advanced) > Automated captions, Full transcript, Save captions are all ON

Check Settings -> Meeting -> In Meeting (Basic) > Sound notification when someone joins or leaves is OFF

Check Settings -> Meeting -> In Meeting (Basic) > Screen sharing > Who can share > All Participants

Check Settings -> Meeting -> In Meeting (Basic) > Hide participant profile pictures in a meeting ON


### **2-3 days before the session**



* Confirm notetaker availability
* Let notetaker know if anything has changed in the conversation guide
* Determine task rotation order based on final sample size 


### **Day of the session**



* Use [#feedback-backchannel](https://dsva.slack.com/messages/C40B45NJK/details/) in Slack for real-time feedback from observers.
* Mute your Slack notifications.
* Send out the [observer instructions](https://depo-platform-documentation.scrollhelp.site/research-design/Observer-guidelines.1622311177.html) to your observers (Slackbot will do this for you if you type "observer instructions" into [#feedback-backchannel](https://dsva.slack.com/channels/feedback-backchannel)).
* Sign into Zoom website via Google as Perigean w/ credentials provided and start Zoom meeting 5-10 minutes before


### **Starting the session**



* In the Attendees section, make sure everyone except the participant is on mute.
* In the Participant "more" menu, make sure "Play Join and Leave Sound" is unchecked
* Check your [screensharing setup] and share permissions for participants.
* When the participant is ready, begin the session with the following intro*

## **1. Intro + consent (3-5 minutes)**

Moderator script (adapt as needed):



* Thanks for joining today. My name is [NAME]. I work on the team that helps improve VA.gov and related VA health tools.
* Today we’re going to talk about healthcare alerts/notifications you may see while managing appointments, messages, medications, or medical records.
* We’re not testing you—if anything feels confusing or frustrating, that’s helpful feedback for us.
* Participation is voluntary and will not impact your benefits.
* In this session, I’m going to ask you to login to your [VA.gov](VA.gov) account and share your screen while you check a few things.  This means that we may see some of your personal information.
* Knowing this, are you comfortable if I record my screen and audio as we talk today? All videos are kept in a secure location and destroyed after analysis.
* Are you comfortable with it if we share anonymized video clips with our VA colleagues for research purposes, provided we remove any information that can be used to identify you?

(If observers are present)



* We also have a couple teammates listening in to help us take notes. Are you okay with that?
* If no: Use Slack to kindly ask the observers to leave the Zoom.
* If yes: Admit observers.
* Then, ask the notetaker and observers to give a brief, camera-on introduction, like their first name and position with VA.gov. (This is meant to humanize the interaction of observation for the participant.)
* Once complete, ask the notetaker and observers to turn off their cameras for the remainder of the session to save bandwidth
* If yes: Once I start recording, I am going to confirm that you are okay with me recording this session once more.

*Start recording and **capture consent ***



* I have started recording. I'd like to confirm: Are you comfortable if I record my screen and the audio as we talk today?
* Are you comfortable if we decide to use a recorded clip from our discussion today to share with other VA partners if we remove any information that can be used to identify you?


## **2. Warm-up: current routines + expectations (2 minutes)**



1. How long have you been using VA healthcare?
2. What VA facilities do you currently use?
3. What facilities have you used in the past?
    * Have you used any VA facility other than [insert facility name]?
4. When you need to manage your VA health care (appointments, messages, prescriptions, test results), what tools do you usually use?
    * VA.gov, My HealtheVet, phone calls, in-person, mobile app, email/text?


## **3. Baseline recall: most recent healthcare communication they remember (2- 3  minutes)**



1. Think back to the most recent alert or update to your VA healthcare access and experience you remember seeing.
    * What did you see? (web alert, mail flyer, poster, etc)
    * Where did you see it? 
    * What did it say (roughly)?
    * What did you do next?
    * What if any questions did you have?

Probes:



* On first read, what did you think it meant?
* Did it tell you what to do and by when?


## **4. Early exposure: review alerts and functionality on their account (screen share) (12 minutes)**

**Expectations/recall **

2. When did you realize you couldn’t do what you expected?
3. What did you understand as your next step?

## **5. Reflection (5 minute)**



1. Thinking about the alerts you looked at today, what would you say the most confusing part?
2. What would you change about the wording?
3. What could the VA say that they did not say that would make you more comfortable?
4. Anything else you want to share that we haven’t covered?
