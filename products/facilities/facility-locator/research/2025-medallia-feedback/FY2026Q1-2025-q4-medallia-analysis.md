## Qualitative
A few patterns emerged in the data. First, many comments were unrelated to facilities products (about 25%). These comments were often general VA or staff feedback but also significant comments about difficulties scheduling appointments. Users may be attempting to use the locator to find the facility they want to schedule at and then running into issues. 
A significant number of respondents (28) seemed to be using Facility locator as essentially an insurance check, particularly for community care providers or to check if a specific doctor was covered, leading to the feature request to search for a doctor by name or have clearer information about what places would take VA. 
Finding Urgent and Emergency care is a consistent pain point and key use case for facility locator (46).
Inaccurate data, particularly for community care providers (urgent care or specific services) is a key driver of disatisfaction and reduces trust (66).
VA employees also have specific use cases for facility locator in how they serve patients and provide them with location data (8).

## Positive Feedback
Primary drivers of positive comments were general VA comments. Specific to Facility locator drivers for positive comments were being able to find needed information (24) and some positive comments about ease of use (32).
