# Research Plan for Rainbows, Post-911 GI Bill Benefits Wizard, 10/08/2018

## Goals
#### 1. What product & team are you doing this research for?
Post-911 GI Bill Benefits page, on the Rainbows team
#### 2. Background: Briefly, what is the background on this product? What would a new person on the team need to know about this product?
The Post-911 GI Bill Benefits page provides a digital statement of benefits that veterans can use to confirm their benefits (if they have applied and received benefits more than 60 days prior) and get more information about how much theyâ€™ve used and how much benefit is remaining.
#### 3. Research questions: What question(s) do you hope to be able to answer after completing this research?
* Can Veterans find this troubleshooting wizard when they need help?
* Does a troubleshooting wizard help Veterans get the information they need?

#### 4. Hypothesis:
A wizard-like troubleshooting feature on the GI Benefits page will help Veterans experiencing errors to understand what went wrong and what their next steps are for solving the problem. Veterans will feel confident about their next steps and help desk calls related to this page will be reduced.

## Method
#### 1.	What method of research are you planning? 
A first click test using Chalkmark on Optimal Sort. The test will be remote and unmoderated.

#### 2.	Why this method? How does this methodology help you answer your research questions? 
I'd like to test whether users are able to identify this new feature through the call-to-action and whether the responses they receive after troubleshooting are helpful and easy to identify.

#### 3.	Where are you planning to do your research?
Through a Chalkmalk first click test: https://adhoc.optimalworkshop.com/chalkmark/gibswizard-0

#### 4.	What will you be testing?
Full visual design prototyped screens of a wizard-like interaction
